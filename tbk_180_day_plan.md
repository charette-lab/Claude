# TBK — The First 180 Days: Launching the Path to 10% Operating Margin in 18 Months

Target: operating profit run-rate ¥6.3bn (≈10.6% margin) by month 18 — the Phase-1 pro-forma.
Required capture: +9% average reprice on the ¥51bn OEM-discounted book (one-third of the stated
30% gap), ~450 of the 1,000 excess factory heads, pruning of negative-contribution lines, with
R&D ramping to the peer run-rate (4.7% of sales) *against* the margin gain.

The 180 days are not for finishing any of this — they are for making the four moves irreversible
and proving the run-rate bridge is real.

## The four workstreams (one owner each, reporting weekly to the CEO)

1. **PRICE** — keiretsu repricing + aftermarket/export realization
2. **FOOTPRINT** — heads, plants, China
3. **PORTFOLIO** — prune the bleeders, grow the aftermarket
4. **PRODUCT** — gated R&D ramp (ADB + e-pump/TCU) with Brakes India

---

## Days 0–30 — Mandate and truth

- **Board mandate locked**: 10% OPM at month 18, the ¥6.3bn bridge as the single scoreboard;
  four workstream owners named; weekly steering installed.
- **One source of truth**: the product/customer marginal-profit packs (the H1 FY2025 format)
  institutionalized monthly, group-wide — every decision below traces to a line in them.
- **Quote freeze**: no new business below a 20% contribution floor without CEO sign-off.
- **Contract map**: every keiretsu contract by product, volume, renewal date, and price vs
  market benchmark; the SAW evidence pack assembled (class-leading performance data + the ~30%
  gap quantification) — the negotiation ammunition.
- **Stop-loss list drafted**: GKN case-set (−30.2%), Suzuki (−1.2%), Ebara (−0.5%), MMTH brake
  parts, O/brake (−4.9%), B/housing (−8.7%), W/P ES (1.7%) — ~¥2bn of sales to exit or reprice.
- **Compel the internal quality evidence** (ownership prerogative): OEM supplier scorecards,
  warranty-claim rates, and PPM defect data from Isuzu, Fuso, and Hino — replacing "probably
  fine, unproven" with hard numbers inside 30 days.
- **Engineering pipeline audit**: how many electric coolant/oil pumps are in A/B sample testing
  with OEMs today; what is the hard homologation timeline for the 19.5" ADB prototype.
- **R&D program leads appointed** for the two gated programs; joint roadmap workshop with
  Brakes India scheduled inside the first 30 days.

## Days 31–60 — Decisions

- **Pruning decisions executed on paper**: exit or ≥15% reprice notices prepared for the
  stop-loss list (12-month implementation window).
- **Footprint blueprint approved**: China fix-or-exit decision (losses both years, depreciation
  at 12.8% of segment sales); Japan consolidation study around SAW-line utilization; voluntary
  retirement program designed — tranche 1 of ~450 heads, restructuring cash budget ¥2–3bn.
- **Pricing playbook v1**: aftermarket price list **+10% effective immediately** (market-priced
  channel; 30.2% CM proves the headroom — worth ~¥240M/yr on its own); SAW renegotiation opened
  with Isuzu and Fuso at the next renewal windows, target +9–12% over two cycles, paired with a
  giveback that costs little: a committed ADB/e-pump technology roadmap (price for supply
  security and future tech, not price for nothing).
- **R&D rebase approved**: FY3/27 plan at ¥2.7–2.8bn (4.7% of sales), gated: ADB ¥0.8bn,
  e-pump/TCU ¥0.9bn, low-dust/corrosion friction ¥0.4bn, core support ¥0.6bn; hiring plan for
  30–40 electromechanical/software engineers.

## Days 61–90 — Execution starts

- First reprice letters out (aftermarket + non-contracted lines); first exits executed.
- Voluntary retirement program launched; China decision announced internally and to partners.
- **ADB pilot-line investment case to the board** (¥4.5–7.5bn phased capex, Brakes India
  co-investment structure); e-pump program converts two development sockets to committed
  programs (ELF EV precharge follow-on; Denso AC ECWP scale-up).
- **KPI cockpit live**: monthly contribution by product/customer, price-capture %, headcount,
  and the OP run-rate bridge vs ¥6.3bn — visible to the board.

## Days 91–180 — Lock-in

- **Keiretsu renegotiations in motion**: at least two of the top-five contracts repriced
  (+6–9% realized on those volumes). One demonstrated walk-away or hard re-quote on a
  negative-margin line — the credibility event that makes every later negotiation cheaper.
  Sequence where TBK is sole-source first (the share map shows which cells).
- **Headcount tranche 1 complete**: ~200–250 heads out by day 180, remainder of the 450 by
  month 12; first plant consolidation announced.
- **R&D at full run-rate**; first patents filed on e-pump/TCU and SAW improvements; JSAE paper
  submitted — start making the innovation externally legible (it currently isn't: 2–3
  patents/yr).
- **Export/aftermarket pilot**: SAW-class drums shipped to India via Brakes India channels at
  market prices — the arbitrage that needs no keiretsu permission.
- **Day-180 board review**: OP run-rate ≥ ¥3.5bn annualized (vs ¥1.5bn start), go/no-go gates
  for phase-2 items.

## The 18-month run-rate trajectory

| Milestone | OP run-rate | Margin | Main drivers landing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | ¥1.5bn | 2.7% | — |
| Day 180 | **≥¥3.5bn** | ~6% | Aftermarket +10%, pruning, first exits, first heads, non-keiretsu pricing |
| Month 12 | ~¥5bn | ~8% | Heads tranche complete, China resolved, first keiretsu renewals |
| Month 18 | **¥6.3bn** | **10.6%** | Second renewal wave, consolidation savings, R&D at full cost absorbed |

Funding — the asset base first, pricing second: the immediate cash lever is the underutilized
footprint (>¥2.2bn of impairments/restructuring already absorbed, China losses ending, dormant
PP&E at 39% of sales monetized via consolidation and disposals), reinforced by the early pricing
wins (aftermarket +10%, stopped bleeders). Together they cover the R&D ramp from roughly month
6; restructuring cash (¥2–3bn) and ADB capex bridge via the Brakes India proceeds (¥1.1bn
already raised), the net-cash balance sheet (0.45x EBITA), and pruned-asset disposals. This
sequencing breaks the funding circularity: no step depends on margins that only future products
can earn.

## Risks and the honest counters

- **OEM retaliation / resourcing threats**: sequence repricing through sole-source cells first;
  always pair price with the technology-roadmap giveback; remember the evidence — non-keiretsu
  customers already pay ~2x, and Isuzu just took a 10%-shareholder seat next to TBK via the
  capital raise context (alignment exists).
- **Social/union**: voluntary program first; Japan consolidation framed around the growth
  investments (ADB line) being located where heads are freed.
- **Bandwidth**: four workstreams only; nothing else gets a steering slot for 180 days.
- **The trap to avoid**: per TBK's own history (negative ROIIC at every horizon), the failure
  mode is spreading effort. The 180-day plan deliberately does fewer things, gated, with the
  bridge as the only scoreboard.
