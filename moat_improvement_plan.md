## What it costs — almost nothing beyond the plan already specified

The striking result: **~80% of the achievable uplift requires no money beyond the existing
program.** The big movers are either free (Ch8 is discipline plus returning cash), self-paying
(Ch5 restructuring recovers through the P&L), or already costed (Ch6/Ch9 ride on the ¥2.7bn/yr
R&D + ADB capex). The genuinely incremental spend:

- **Legibility program (Ch2): ~¥0.15bn/yr** — patents, publications, certifications, award pursuit
- **Aftermarket/ecosystem build (Ch4): ~¥1-2bn over 3 years** — network, SKUs, India channel
- **Opportunistic buybacks (Ch8): ¥1-3bn** — at 1.5-2.2x phase-1 earnings, each ¥1bn retires
  ~10% of the market cap; nothing in the entire plan creates moat-score evidence (and per-share
  value) faster

Total incremental: **~¥2-5bn over 3 years** on top of the ~¥8bn/3yr R&D program and one-off
items already in the 180-day plan — for ~1.2 points of moat score and a band change.

## The three best options, stated plainly

1. **Allocation discipline + buybacks (Ch8)** — biggest single gain (+0.35), negative cost,
   and it compounds: every other chapter's evidence becomes credible once capital stops being
   destroyed. The market is selling TBK's own moat points at 1.5-2.2x earnings.
2. **Footprint (Ch5)** — +0.25 for money the P&L pays back anyway; already in the 180-day plan.
3. **Legibility (Ch2)** — +0.15 for ~¥0.15bn/yr; the highest return-on-effort lever and the one
   that makes the export/Brakes India strategy sellable.

The expensive lever — the R&D program behind Ch6/Ch9 — is necessary but already committed for
P&L reasons; its moat-score contribution (+0.5 combined) comes free on top.

## Honest limits

- Ch3 (hegemony) is the ceiling, not the lever: 8.0 can only be defended. The ADB program's
  real moat value is preventing the UD outcome (all cells lost) from repeating at Isuzu/Fuso.
- Ch4 (ecosystem) realistically caps at ~4-5: TBK will never own a Knorr-style EBS/data network;
  the TCU software layer plus aftermarket annuity is the achievable version.
- Ch8 cannot be bought, only demonstrated — the score follows 2-3 years of ROIIC evidence,
  which is why it starts at "first buyback/pruning executed," not at a presentation.
- Crossing 7.8 (Compounder Target) is not realistic in this industry — no peer is there;
  Concentric's 6.98 is the practical frontier, and the 5-year path reaches it.
