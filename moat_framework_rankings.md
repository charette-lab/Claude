## Read-across for the TBK thesis

1. **TBK (5.38) ranks 9th of 13 — below every Western peer except ZF and Mikuni, above only
   the distressed/displaced names.** Its score decomposes into a real moat (Ch1+Ch3 contribute
   2.33 of 5.38) being drained by the compounding engine (Ch8+Ch9 contribute just 0.50 of a
   possible 2.00).
2. **The gap to Concentric (6.98) is almost entirely Phases 2-3** — substitution positioning and
   capital allocation — not moat structure. Concentric converted the same product category into
   a Watchlist-grade compounder by leading the e-pump transition and allocating at 19-29% ROE.
   That is the execution delta, quantified at ~1.6 framework points.
3. **What would move TBK's score**: demonstrated positive ROIIC on the new programs (Ch8: 2→5
   adds +0.30), e-pump/ADB socket wins converting the runway from theoretical to scaling (Ch9:
   3→6 adds +0.30), and externally legible quality/IP (Ch2: 6→7 adds +0.10). Realistic ceiling
   on a 3-5 year view: ~6.4 — Watchlist boundary. The moat is not the problem; the engine is.

## Method caveats

- Scores for the five pre-ranked names are taken from the dataset, not re-derived; chapter-level
  detail for them is not available to me, so cross-company chapter comparisons use my scores only.
- TBK's Ch3 rests on analyst-sourced share figures (the 70%/90% provenance caveat applies).
- Ch8 scores follow the methodology's Domain B rule (80% external): the dataset's ROIIC/RR is
  treated as authoritative over any internal narrative, including TBK's FY3/26 improvement story,
  which is one year old.
- Single-analyst application; the methodology's optimism/detraction blending was applied
  qualitatively, not as a computed two-source average.
