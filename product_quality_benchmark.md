# Product Quality, Innovation, TCO & Service: TBK vs Peers — Evidence Review

Compiled 2026-06-12 from trusted, preferentially adversarial sources: NHTSA recall filings (which
name component suppliers explicitly), customer-granted OEM quality awards, independent reader/fleet
surveys, patent registries, and the user dataset's moat scores. Self-claimed material is labeled
weak throughout.

## Evidence-quality hierarchy used

1. **Adversarial records** (strongest): NHTSA Part 573 equipment recalls — supplier named by law
2. **Customer-granted**: OEM supplier quality awards (PACCAR 10PPM, Daimler Masters of Quality)
3. **Independent surveys**: ETM Awards (German fleet/driver reader vote), AMS reader votes
4. **Output proxies**: patent registries, documented product firsts, technical-society awards
5. **Self-claimed** (weakest): install bases, "industry-leading" service/warranty claims
6. **Structural gap**: Japanese MLIT recalls and OEM supplier awards do NOT name suppliers
   publicly — Japanese suppliers (TBK, Mikuni) can be neither validated nor indicted this way

## What this means

1. **Quality (conformance):** The adversarial record suggests TBK's manufacturing quality is
   probably fine — 75 years supplying Isuzu/Hino/Fuso with zero findable recalls or scandals,
   in a country where supplier scandals (Akebono's falsification, others) get heavy coverage.
   But "probably fine" is the ceiling the evidence supports: unlike Bendix (PPM-validated by
   PACCAR) or Meritor (Daimler-awarded), **TBK has no external quality validation at all**.
   Note also that the Western leaders' recalls are not disqualifying — Bendix runs the largest
   install base AND the largest recall; scale produces both.
2. **Innovation:** The starkest gap in the whole study. Knorr's ~2,900 patent families vs TBK's
   2-3 grants/year independently confirms the capitalized-R&D finding (60-130x stock gap) from
   a completely different data source. Even distressed Akebono out-innovates TBK on observable
   output (JSME award, EMB development), and Mikuni's e-pump win is patent-backed. TBK's
   innovation is real but invisible: retarders since the 1960s, e-pumps and a TCU in development,
   an ADB prototype — none of it externally validated or protected at scale.
3. **TCO & service:** Brand-level TCO data does not publicly exist (fleets don't publish it;
   IMR's brand studies are paywalled) — the honest answer is that TCO standing must be inferred
   from proxies: install base (Bendix ~85% of NA air-disc wheel ends), OEM contract extensions
   (Meritor/DTNA), and service networks (Knorr's pan-EMEA certified network vs TBK's
   Japan/Thailand footprint). On every proxy, the systems players lead and TBK doesn't appear.
4. **The moat scores in the user dataset align with the external evidence**: Knorr 7.55 at the
   top (validated quality + patent fortress + service network), Akebono 4.95 at the bottom
   (documented quality events + distress), TBK unscored — fittingly, since TBK is *unmeasurable*
   from outside: no awards, no surveys, no recalls, minimal patents. Invisibility cuts both ways:
   it protects the domestic franchise today and makes export/new-customer expansion (the Brakes
   India route) harder, because there is no third-party evidence to sell on.
5. **Strategic read-across**: TBK's quality reputation is an asset held entirely inside the
   keiretsu relationships — exactly like its market share. The R&D catch-up program should
   therefore include cheap reputation-capitalization steps: patenting the e-pump/TCU and ADB
   work it already does (Mikuni shows the value), publishing at JSAE, and pursuing external
   quality validation (OEM awards, certifications made public) to make its quality legible to
   customers beyond Isuzu/Hino/Fuso.

## Key gaps (honest)

- Japanese OEM supplier-award winner lists and MLIT supplier attribution are not public — TBK
  and Mikuni cannot be benchmarked the way US/EU suppliers can.
- IMR-type fleet brand-preference data is paywalled; no public brand-level TCO or CSA-by-brand
  data exists.
- TBK's patent subject-matter breakdown needs a direct J-PlatPat query (registry page exists).
- The TBK ADB exhibit year is unresolved (2019 vs 2022 sources conflict).
- Brembo's claimed PACE award is unverified; no PACE award confirmed for any company in the set.
